Report Reference: PHE 39287

Description/ history: report of severe flu-like outbreak at St. Bride's Primary School, Tovington. 3 children showing acute symptoms.

Sample(s) taken: naso-pharyngeal swabs from acute phase patients

Patient NameJonny B.Ann I.Jamal O.
Age10811
Sample NameJBANAIANJOAN
Methods

QuickVue Influenza A+B Test, 10 minute assay for presence of viral antigens.
Sensitivity for Naso-pharyngeal swabs: Influenza A = 83%, B = 62%.
Specificity for Naso-pharyngeal swabs: Influenza A = 89%, B = 98%.

Results
  • All three samples gave a positive result (red band) for Influenza A
  • All three samples gave a negative result (no band) for Influenza B
  • All three assays gave a good negative control band (blue band) so the assay worked.
Conclusions

All three children were suffering from an acute infection that the QuickVue assay indicated was caused by Influenza A. Follow up should involve uncovering whether this infection is of a seasonal variety or something more concerning (e.g. bird flu).
